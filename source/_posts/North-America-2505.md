The original plan for this trip was fly to Toronto, spend some time at Bruce Peninsula National Park, view Niagara Falls at Canada side, then cross the board and view Niagara Falls at the US side, and drive to NYC. However, the return ticket to NYC was much cheaper, so the plan was changed.

## Day 1. Shanghai - Hong Kong - JFK

It's a long trip from Asia to East US. For a direct flight, it takes about 15 hours. We had connection at Hong Kong, so it took even longer time. It's lucky to had a good view for the Great Lakes from the air plane. A clear boundary between the lake and the land.

One thing I have to say is the hotels in NYC are so expensive.

![1](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/SMartQi/Live-the-Life/master/source/gallery/na2025/1-1.jpg)

## Day 2. JFK - Ithaca

We rented a Jeep Ranger in 2019 and it was good, so we rented a Jeep Ranger again this time. Surprisingly, this was a 4XE version, hybrid vehicle. However, it didn't support lane change assistance. Driving in NYC was hard, especially for people haven't been NYC before. So many cars, complicated roads, also traffic jam on I87. When we arrived at Taughannock Falls State Park, it was quite late. And it started to rain when we were walking to the fall. We had to return because we didn't take the umbrella. Then we drove to another viewpoint, and looked at the fall from a higher and far position.

## Day 3. Ithaca - Buffalo

Not far away from Ithaca, we walked the trails in Buttermilk Falls State Park. Although we didn't walk to the end of the trail, the beautiful scenery was unforgettable. It's not majestic like the Niagara Falls, but it's quite and colorful. It's especially beautiful when the sun shines on the lake. Then we went to the Watkins Glen State Park. While walking along the stream, we saw a lot of water falls and cliffs. We also walked behind some of the water falls. After visiting two state parks, we stopped by the Corning Museum. Its glass products are very famous. We purchased its tableware many years ago and they're still in use. We then went to Genesee Falls on our way to Buffalo. We were lucky to see a train on the bridge, but don't have the time to take a picture.

## Day 4. Buffalo - Niagara Falls City

We finally saw the Niagara Falls. We took the Maid of the Mist boat tour for getting closer to the fall. Heavy wind and water made me hard to clearly see the water fall. However, it's a good experience to feel the water fall. There is also a trail close to the water fall, which provides the opportunity to stand in the front of the water fall. After feeling the Niagara Falls from the US side, we walked across the Rainbow Bridge, to the Niagara Falls City, Ontario, Canada. We booked a room with falls view so we can stay in the room and watch the Niagara Falls on the chair. The room was good, we had a relax afternoon and evening. We also had the chance to see the firework and the water fall with difference colors of lights.

## Day5. Niagara Falls City - Buffalo

At Canada side, there is a tunnel behind the fall and a deck very close to the fall. We closely experienced strong wind and water there. My waterproof jacket is good, but my shoes wet again. We also learned the history of the power station. The bus ticket is a day pass, so we took the bus to the Whirlpool Trail, which is the north side of the river. In the afternoon, we back to the US via the Rainbow Bridge.
Buffalo wing is famous, so we found a restaurant which is good at it. I should choose all flats for the wings. Although it's a little bit expensive, I think it worth. Anyway, the wing tasted good.

## Day6. Buffalo - Camillus

We head back to New York City, but we'd like to choose a different way so we have different views. In Fort Niagara State Park, a map says we can see Toronto Skyline from here if the weather is good. It's so close. In Green Lake State Park, we walked around the lake and relaxed on the lounge chairs by the lake. The air was fresh and the lake was peace and beautiful.

## Day7. Camillus - NYC

On our way to NYC, we had a rest stop which supposed to be a viewpoint. However, when we drove here, it's likely to be small park. But it's a good place to take a rest and have lunch. Before arriving NYC, we walked the trail in Minnewaska State Park to Sam's Point. We stood on a large rock, good view to the park. MM told me a lot of people go here for maple in fall, so I started to imagine how it looks like if these trees are red. We returned the car and arrived Manhattan at about 8pm. The hotel in Manhattan was expensive and the room was not big. But we stayed in a hotel close to the Time Square.

![12](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/SMartQi/Live-the-Life/master/source/gallery/na2025/7-1.jpg)

## Day8. NYC

Today's schedule was quite flexible. We firstly went to the Lyric Theatre in the morning as we were going to watch a play tomorrow. Then we visited some famous places in New York City, Time Square, Central Park, The High Line. We spent a long time to find an entry to The High Line. We had a Frappuccino in the Hudson Yards. It's expensive than I thought, but it felt good to have a drink and take a seat in the city.

## Day9. NYC

This was the day MM liked. We watched Harry Potter stage play the Lyric Theatre. Lucky for us, we won the lottery so we spent less money to have very good seats. When I received the email notification of winning the lottery, we were so surprised even doubted if it's a spam. We redeemed the tickets early in the morning. In case it's not true, we can purchase two tickets in the office.

NYSE is the place where many people like taking photos. I took some photos there and back to broadway so we won't miss the show. We had a lobster in the noon then waited for the show. Before the show, the theatre lobby was opened for shopping, taking photos. It also sold the famous Butterbeer. We enjoyed the show, especially in such a short distance.

![15](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/SMartQi/Live-the-Life/master/source/gallery/na2025/9-1.jpg)

## Day 10 NYC

Today's focus was Statue of Liberty. Our boat departed from Battery park. The queue was not long. We got on the boat quite quickly. The boat was a good place to take pictures for the Statue of Liberty. It cruses in the front of the statue, kept a good distance.

We didn't have the ticket to the crown of the statue, but we had pedestal access. We went to the pedestal by stairs, walked around the pedestal. The statue was so large, just stand in front of me. After taking a lot of pictures, we purchased some gifts in the gift shop and also a postcard.

Our old plan was took the boat to NJ to take a look then back to NYC, but it took too long time so we then back to the Battery park and sent out the postcard in a post office.
